### Investment Opportunity: Cong Ty Kim Va Gordon – Revolutionizing Small-Scale Poultry with The Chicken Fortress

**Company Overview**  
Cong Ty Kim Va Gordon is an innovative ag-tech startup based in Tay Ninh, Vietnam, developing modular, sustainable poultry systems for small to mid-scale farmers. Our flagship product, **The Chicken Fortress**, transforms standard shipping containers into self-contained, high-efficiency habitats for 100 laying hens. We combine vermiculture-based waste recycling, and pasture access to drastically reduce operating costs, eliminate odors, and create a circular protein system—turning waste into feed. No direct competitors provide this full combination of odor-free vermiculture + insect recycling + pasture integration in a portable, container-based unit.

**Unique Value Proposition & Competitive Advantage**  
The Chicken Fortress is the only integrated system on the market offering:
- **Closed-loop vermiculture waste processing**: Chicken manure is fully consumed by worms and native flies, producing zero detectable odors and converting waste into high-protein insect larvae (fly/worm) that chickens consume directly.
- **20% feed savings** from recycled insect protein.
- **Additional 30% feed reduction** via daytime pasture access through automated electric doors, enabling natural foraging.


This results in dramatically lower input costs, premium "pasture-raised, zero-waste" eggs, and valuable byproducts (worm castings for sale as organic fertilizer).

**Product Features**  
- Capacity: 100 laying hens.  
- Infrastructure: Roosting area with 18 roll-away nesting boxes to minimize egg contamination.  
- Waste management: Slatted flooring directs manure to vermiculture zone below; flies consume waste pre-ammonia, larvae auto-harvest into chicken area for protein boost.  
- Automation: Electric powered doors for timed pasture access; gravity-fed feed/water via PVC to troughs/stations.  
- User-friendly: Dedicated clean maintenance room for egg collection, feed/water restocking—minimal chicken disturbance or manure contact.  
- Logistics: Designed for easy shipping (container-compatible), freight truck, or rail; minimal on-site installation required.

**Financial Model & ROI for End-Users (Farmers)**  
A conventional factory layer hen consumes ~42 kg of feed annually (~115 g/day). At ~$0.45/kg bulk feed cost, that's ~$19/hen/year in feed alone.

With The Chicken Fortress:  
- Feed usage drops to ~21 kg/hen/year (50%+ reduction via insect recycling + foraging).  
- Feed cost: ~$9.50/hen/year.  
- Purchase cost of Hen: $10
- Vermiculture byproduct: ~35 kg worm castings/hen/year, sold wholesale at ~$0.35/kg = ~$12 revenue/hen/year.  
- Egg output: 250 eggs/hen/year. At conservative farm-gate price of $0.20/egg (premium pasture-raised) = $50 revenue/hen/year.  
- **Total revenue per hen**: ~$62/year (eggs + castings).  
- **Net profit per hen**: ~$42/year (after feed and minimal other costs).  
- **Per unit (100 hens)**: ~$4,200 net profit/year.

At a retail price of **$TBD per unit**, end-users achieve **~28% annual ROI** (payback in ~4 years), competitive with or superior to traditional factory farms (typically 20–30% ROI) but on a smaller, more flexible scale with sustainability advantages. Small farms gain corporate-level efficiency without massive infrastructure.

**Market Opportunity**

Small/mid-scale farmers struggle against industrial giants; The Chicken Fortress levels the playing field with lower costs, zero-waste credentials, and portable scalability.
